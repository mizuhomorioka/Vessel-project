Vessels heading to Japan

That's a lot of dots. This is because Japan relies on imports in many field including its energy resources.

According to a Japanese goverment's report, in 2021, 83.2% of Japan's primary energy was fossil fuels. Of that, 25.4% was coal, 36.3% petroleum, and 21.5% LNG. But Japan gets very little of its resources. About 90% of fossil fuels are imported.

In particular, dependence on the Middle East for oil has always been noted: 91.9% of oil is imported from Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Kuwait, and Qatar. The sea routes of tankers are similar: from the Middle East, through the Indian Ocean, through Southeast Asia, and then to Japan. As you can see the chart below, it is more linear than ships carrying coal.

Coal is imported from a variety of places

Dependence on imports for energy could be a risk for Japan. This is the first figure, focused on Asia. As you can see, many ships pass through South China Sea. It is surrounded by China, Taiwan, the Philippines, Malaysia, Brunei, Indonesia, Singapore, Thailand, Cambodia, and Vietnam, and there is a constant struggle for territorial sovereignty.

The nine-dash line, the boundary line of the South China Sea claimed by China, is also located here. The news that the movie "Barbie" is banned in Vietnam because of the appearance of the nine-dash line is still fresh in our memories.

The Taiwan Strait issue also has been strained. When something happen, which sea route would be used? Since oil is till an important resource in JapanIt it is worth keeping in mind that oil is being transported through risks.
